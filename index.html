About

Other than video games, I haven't found anything that I have been passionate about. However, when I worked as an IT technician, I discovered my passion for tech-related things. This led me to pursue a degree in computer science.

During my studies, I enjoyed learning the theory and practical aspects. However, I needed a speciality since I enjoyed all aspects of the course.

My efforts were all over the place, evident in my course choices during my third year. These were artificial intelligence, data visualisation, cybersecurity and machine learning.

Web development

Visual Aspect

When I started coding, I learnt to code. I enjoyed making the computer do things I wanted. However, something was missing as most things were output in the terminal or console.
The abstraction is not a problem, but with web development, I can view my changes in real-time and see results in a visual representation which I find more appealing.

Problem Solving

I was taught that computer scientists are not coders or programmers but problem solvers. Languages are tools to solve problems.
Web development encompasses many aspects, including logic, design, user interaction, data, etc. Exposure to many aspects is exciting as I can learn from different fields and improve my skills.

Tangability

When I create things from scratch or fix existing problems, there is visual evidence of my work which I love having. I have always wanted to have a hobby in which I could keep a record and see the improvements over time.

Founders and Coders

Why I am interested in applying

For my final project at university, I decided to make a web app which sparked my interest in web development. Web development balances visual feedback with the abstract side of coding.

After leaving university with little success and confidence in my abilities, I started looking at boot camps to upskill and find a speciality.

I am applying to FaC (Founders and Coders) to help me transition into web development. While I have a good foundation, I need more area-specific knowledge and guidance, which FaC could be.

Founders and Coders instead of other boot camps

One of the things I miss is having peers to learn from and to add extra motivation to improve so I can keep up with more talented developers. Joining a boot camp would add another slight push towards my goal.

Another aspect which interests me is the mentoring aspect which is incredibly valuable as people who work in the industry can lend experiences to help inexperienced people.

Compared to other boot camps, I have yet to see one that requires interviews and projects; however, this is an advantage as it pushes prospects to try learning themselves, see if it is enjoyable, and show eagerness.
